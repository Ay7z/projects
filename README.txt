* IPL Cricket Analytics

This project analyzes IPL ball by ball delivery data from 2008 to 2020 using Python and Pandas to uncover performance trends across batters, bowlers and teams.

** Dataset
The dataset contains match level and ball by ball delivery data spanning 13 IPL seasons. A sample of 50,000 deliveries was used for this analysis alongside the 50,000 rows of matches dataset.

** Questions Answered
1. Who scored the most runs overall across all IPL seasons?
2. Who took the most wickets across all IPL seasons?
3. Which bowler bowled the most dot balls?
4. Which player won the most Player of the Match awards?
5. Which team won the most matches across all seasons?
6. Which batsman hit the most sixes?
7. Which bowler has the best economy rate?
8. Which batsman has the highest six rate per ball faced?

** Key Insights
SK Raina emerged as the most consistent run scorer, his volume pointing to a player who batted regularly in high pressure middle order situations across multiple seasons rather than having one standout year.

IK Pathan led in wickets with 64 closely followed by RP Singh with 63. RP Singh also topped the dot balls chart with 462, confirming he was among IPL's most economical and threatening bowlers across the dataset.

AB de Villiers leads Player of the Match awards with 25, with all top 5 being batsmen. This suggests match winning innings are valued more than bowling performances when awarding POTM.

Mumbai Indians lead team wins with 144, built on depth and consistency rather than dependence on any single match winner.

Among batsmen with 200 plus balls faced, Yusuf Pathan hits a six every 10 balls with CH Gayle in third place confirming his reputation as the most destructive hitter in IPL history.

Among bowlers with 120 plus balls bowled, R Ashwin and SL Malinga appearing in the top 5 confirms their status as the most economical bowlers in IPL history.
